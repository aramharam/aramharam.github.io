राम राम, I'm Ram, a firm votary of GNU/LINUX from the deserts of Rajasthan. This is my website on the Internet and here I will try to express my thoughts freely and lucidly.


A little preface to my blogs and on my selection of articles that I think would be good for a new visitor. There can be some misrepresentations of facts, stories, folklores, and data but I promise I will try to write my sources wherever it's necessary. I'm doing this to improve my writing skills and organize all of my notes in one place.

Mostly my rambling will wander around Politics, Philosophy, and Medieval Indian history characters. I'm also thinking about writing a diary for a long time, let's see if I stay motivated.

Over the course of the last 3 years, I have come across some cool websites which can be useful to you. You can check them out in the bookmarks section.

Now, I'm also maintaining a list of the media that I'm consooming in the preferences section. If you have any suggestions or recommendations for me, you can message me over here.

The Christian churches also sided with Hitler because communism was a common enemy.

The poor and unemployed approved of Hitler because he created policies that gave them food and other basic necessities in a dire time (the depression).

Youth who were brought up in the Hitler youth (propaganda) were fanatical. They were empowered by the Nazi State and repressed those who were less than absolutely supportive of fascism.

Many older people (above school age) were a little ore critical of the Nazi state, but of course you had to be quiet about this.

Many Germans were very bitter about the Treaty of Versailles, so Hitler was was also a good channel for that anger.

Some people were supportive of Hitler's desire to promote Germany until the war became reality.
